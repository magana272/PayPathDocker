Figure Backlog — Proposed Additional Visualizations

1. Savings Rate Over Time (Line Chart)
   What: Monthly savings rate (surplus / net income * 100) plotted over 6–12 months.
   Why: Lets the user see whether their savings habits are improving or declining,
        not just the current snapshot.
   Data: GET /summary (monthly_surplus, taxes.monthly_net). Needs a historical
         endpoint or the client can compute from monthly cashflow snapshots.

2. Expense Category Breakdown (Donut/Bar Chart)
   What: Expenses grouped by category (rent, utilities, food, subscriptions, etc.)
         showing share of total monthly spend.
   Why: Identifies which spending categories dominate and where cuts would have
        the most impact.
   Data: GET /expenses. Would benefit from a category field on each expense;
         currently expenses have only a name and cost.

3. Income vs. Expenses Trend (Dual-Line Chart)
   What: Monthly total income and total expenses plotted side by side over time.
   Why: Makes it immediately obvious whether the gap between earning and spending
        is widening or narrowing.
   Data: GET /summary (monthly_gross, monthly_expenses). Needs historical monthly
         snapshots or a new endpoint: GET /history?months=12.

4. Debt Composition (Stacked Bar / Treemap)
   What: Current debt balances broken down by type (credit card, auto loan,
         student loan) showing proportion of total.
   Why: Quick visual of which debt categories are largest; complements the
         payoff timeline by showing the starting picture.
   Data: GET /debts (balance, type per account). Already available.

5. Interest-to-Principal Ratio per Payment (Stacked Bar)
   What: For each monthly payment, how much goes to interest vs. principal,
         shown over the payoff timeline.
   Why: Motivates extra payments — users see early payments are mostly interest
         and can visualize the crossover point.
   Data: Derivable from lib/simulate.js avalanche simulation. Track interest
         and principal components per month.

6. Net Worth Over Time (Area Chart)
   What: Total assets (liquid accounts) minus total liabilities (debts) plotted
         monthly.
   Why: The single most important personal-finance metric; showing its trajectory
         is more motivating than a static number.
   Data: GET /liquid + GET /debts. Needs historical snapshots for a true timeline;
         a projection can be computed from cashflow + payoff simulations.

7. Bill Calendar Heatmap
   What: A year-view heatmap (like GitHub contribution graph) colored by net
         cash flow per day — green for income-heavy days, red for bill-heavy days.
   Why: Reveals monthly cash flow patterns at a glance: which weeks are tight,
         which are comfortable.
   Data: GET /calendar (year-wide) or GET /cashflow?days=365.

8. Emergency Fund Runway (Gauge / Progress Bar)
   What: Current liquid balance expressed as months of expenses it would cover
         (liquid / monthly_expenses).
   Why: A core financial health metric. Standard advice is 3–6 months; showing
         the user where they stand gives a concrete savings target.
   Data: GET /summary (monthly_expenses) + GET /liquid (total balance).
         Already available, just needs a visual.

9. Debt-to-Income Ratio Trend (Line with Target Zone)
   What: DTI ratio plotted over time with shaded zones (green < 30%, yellow
         30-40%, red > 40%).
   Why: Lenders use DTI to evaluate creditworthiness; tracking it helps users
         know when they can qualify for better rates.
   Data: GET /summary (dti). Needs historical monthly values.

10. Pay Day Cash Flow Waterfall
    What: Waterfall chart starting from a paycheck amount, subtracting each
          bill/expense that lands before the next paycheck, ending at the
          remaining balance.
    Why: Shows exactly how a single paycheck gets consumed, making it easy to
         spot which bills eat the most.
    Data: GET /calendar (events between two paydays) + paycheck amount from
          GET /income.
